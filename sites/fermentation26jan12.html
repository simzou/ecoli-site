Mixed Acid Fermentation

Fermentation is a cellular process for metabolizing carbon compounds to obtain cell energy in the absence of electron acceptors (i.e., without O2, nitrate, nitrite, sulfate, iron, etc!). ATP is made solely by Substrate Level Phosphorylation.

Key Concepts 

  • Fermentation pathways vary among microorganisms; E. coli operates mixed acid fermentation.
  • Glycolysis pathway derived NADH is oxidized to NAD+ by final steps of fermentation.
  • Each pathway type has characteristic pathway enzymes and end products.
  • Substrate level phosphorylation generates the majority of ATP.
  • Expression of the mixed acid fermentation pathway genes is regulated. Key signals are anaerobiosis, NO3-, glucose, pyruvate and formate.

Specific information on E. coli Mixed Acid Fermentation

The E. coli mixed acid fermentation pathway:

E. coli carries out a mixed acid fermentationwhen grown on glucose without oxygen or any other electron acceptor present.  During anaerobic conditions, glucose is initially metabolized to pyruvate via the glycolysis pathway.

            glucose → 2 pyruvate

Pyruvate is subsequently converted to one or more of the following end products: lactate, acetate, ethanol, succinate, formate, CO2 and H2 by one or more alternative pathways. 

Individual fermentation reactions: 

  • Lactate fermentation
  • Pyruvate cleavage to acetyl-CoA and formate
  • Ethanol fermentation  
  • Acetic acid fermentation
  • Hydrogen and carbon dioxide formation
  • The mixed acid fermentation pathway includes fumarate respiration
